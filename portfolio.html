FLIR Systems, Brysen Relocation

Project Name: FLIR Systems, Brysen Relocation

Location: Wilsonville, OR

Developer/Owner: N/A

Architect: LRS Architects

General Contractor: Commercial Contractors, Inc.

Scope of Work: Preparing facility for lens coating equipment

Overview:

FLIR Systems is the leading manufacturer of thermal and stabilizing camera systems. Partnering with Commercial Contractors in our role as a design build mechanical contractor, Hunter-Davisson was challenged to prepare FLIR’s headquarters in Wilsonville for lens coating equipment being relocated from Florida. Work included placement of a 2,100 lb. chiller on the roof and completing major remodeling inside the facility, while remaining invisible to production staff at FLIR. One of Hunter-Davisson’s biggest accomplishments of this project was using innovative solutions to ensure that the water tanks wouldn’t freeze in the winter without the use of antifreeze.

Highlights:

  • Remodel of facility to include a new mechanical room
  • Extension of existing compressed air piping and AR/N2 gas lines; addition of reverse osmosis (RO) water piping and AR/N2 gas lines
  • Installation of rooftop chiller with water mains for process piping, and branch return and supply lines
  • Design and installation of customized system delivering various water temps to critical processing equipment
  • Design and installation of system to ensure liquid/gas purity
  • Fast turnaround of design and adaptation to client’s production schedule

  • Letter of Recommendation (Neudorfer Engineers, Inc. – Mike Vawter)

Jackson Hall

Project Name: Jackson Hall at Portland Community College

Location: Portland, Oregon

Architect: N/A

General Contractor: Walsh Construction Company

Owner: Portland Community College

Scope of Work:

  • New Air Handler
  • Variable air volume (VAV) system
  • New Chiller
  • New Heat Recovery System

Overview:

Jackson Hall was originally built on Portland Community College’s (PCC) Cascade campus in 1985. Jackson Hall was remodeled in 2003, adding 17,500 square feet of space that contained six new science laboratories and four new classrooms.

Highlights:

  • $6.2 Million addition and remodel adding 17,500 square feet
  • Addition of six new laboratories and four new classrooms
  • Allows for 100% outside air for first stage of cooling
  • 140 Ton Chiller
  • Variable Air Volume (VAV) Terminal Boxes
  • Chemical hood exhaust system with heat recovery
  • DDC Control System

Mary’s Woods

Project Name: Mary’s Woods at Marylhurst

Location: Lake Oswego, Oregon

Architect: N/A

General Contractor: Andersen Construction Company

Overview:

Mary’s Woods at Marylhurst was an extremely advanced and ambitious project that elegantly blended the latest eldercare technologies with modern philosophies surrounding the expanding field of retirement facilities. The plans for the new community improvements called for the addition of 266 independent living apartments, 33 villas, an on–site health care facility with 50 assisted living units and 45 health care beds, a center commons, and a wellness center. To accommodate the expansion, the existing Provincial House (built in 1910) required extensive remodeling and four buildings had to be constructed to house the 33 villas.

Highlights:

  • Hunter-Davisson was selected to oversee and manage the mechanical work for the entire project.
  • By working directly with our general contractor team and several design and engineering teams, Hunter-Davisson was able to provide a total design package on the mechanical systems for the large-scale renovation of the Provincial House.
  • Hunter-Davisson also added design recommendations and suggestions for the four new buildings.

P.P.C. Facility

P.P.C. Facility

Project Name: Portland Public Communication Facility

Location: Portland, Oregon

Owner: City of Portland

Engineer: System Design Consultants

General Contractor: Walsh Construction Company

Scope of Work: HVAC work for newly remodeled 911 building

Overview:

In 2002, the Portland Public Communication building, the facility that houses 911 emergency services, required fairly routine renovations and additions that included new computer rooms, a new maintenance shop, and renovations and expansion to the existing office areas throughout the building. Considering that all emergency services for several large communities rely on the 911 emergency services in this building, no construction-related system or personnel disruptions would be acceptable. During the construction, through extensive coordination and planning, we were able to seamlessly transfer all mechanical systems without any break in emergency services.

 

Highlights:

  • Through our ‘Value Engineering’ service, we were able to recognize significant savings by using varying equipment manufacturers
  • Extensive coordination and planning to ensure zero downtime and/or distractions for emergency services personnel
  • 7000 hours on the project without a single, recordable accident
